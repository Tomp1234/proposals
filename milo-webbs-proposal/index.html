The question you asked

Why Milo and not Copilot. Answered head-on.

Paul, you said this is the question that decides whether the board moves on this. Here are the four reasons, structured so any board member can read them in two minutes. Short version: Copilot is brilliant at Office documents. It is not the tool for asking your business questions of your business data.

01 / Surface area

Copilot reads your inbox. Milo reads Business Central.

Copilot
Indexes Microsoft 365: Outlook, Teams chat, OneDrive, SharePoint. Some integrations into Dynamics. It does not natively answer questions of your sales cube, your inventory cube, your finance SQL.
Milo
Connects directly to Business Central, the three cubes you've already built, Power BI semantic models, and xoppers when it lands. One conversation across all of it.
For Webbs: the stock + PO question on the floor lives in Business Central, not Office 365. That's the whole point.
02 / Context

Copilot guesses your rules. Milo learns them once.

Copilot
Has no way to know that "available" at Webbs means floor stock plus open POs net of allocations, or that the category hierarchy nests seeds under horticulture. You teach it every time.
Milo
A data studio where you encode Webbs' rules once. Trading day windows, category hierarchies, GM definitions, pre-order logic. Every question after that uses them.
For Webbs: the rules in your head that make BC reports correct are the same rules Milo needs. Encoded once, never re-taught.
03 / Citations

Copilot tells you what you want. Milo shows the row.

Copilot
Confidently confident. As you noticed on the call, it leans into the answer it thinks you want. For board-level numbers, the first hallucination is the last time the chairman uses it.
Milo
Every answer cites the table, the filters, the rows. Paul or Ollie can audit any reply, full trace from question to data. If something looks off, you can see why.
For Webbs: trust gets built or broken in the first board meeting. Citations mean you bet the answer.
04 / Permissions

Copilot uses M365 licenses. Milo enforces your business rules.

Copilot
Permission is whatever Office 365 says you can see. There's no concept of "store manager sees their own store" or "HR data ringfences to four named people".
Milo
Role-scoped data access. Cobham's manager sees Cobham. HR data is locked to Paul, Ollie, and two others. Every question is logged, GDPR-clean. SOC2 + ISO 27001.
For Webbs: Ollie's GDPR ringfence isn't a feature request to us. It's how the platform is built.
